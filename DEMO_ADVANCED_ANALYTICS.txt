--- DEMO FLOW ---

Step 2: Let the page load — TF.js initialises automatically.
        Point to the status indicator: "TensorFlow.js ready — backend: webgl"
Step 3: Point to the "How the AI works" info box briefly.

        (Takes ~5–10 seconds — let it run naturally, don't skip.)

        Show the recommended actions list inside the card.
        Show the confidence bar on each card.

--- WHAT TO HIGHLIGHT ---

  - TF.js badge in the header (shows it's a real ML library)
  - Status bar showing the backend (webgl = GPU acceleration)
  - Threshold bar: static reference showing score ranges for each risk level
  - Summary cards: counts AND percentage of staff in each category
  - Alert cards: current score, predicted score, change, confidence bar
  - Recommended actions tailored to each risk level
  - Low/Stable collapsed section (clean UI — not cluttered with safe staff)
  - Re-run button: models retrain fresh each time

--- SCRIPT ---

"This is the Predictive Performance Alerts feature — one of our four
innovative features. What makes this unique is that it uses TensorFlow.js,
Google's machine learning library, running entirely inside the browser.
There is no Python server, no external API — the neural network trains
and predicts right here in real time.

For each staff member with at least three years of historical KPI data,
the system builds and trains a small dense neural network. The model learns
the trend pattern from their yearly scores and predicts what their
performance will look like in the coming year.

This threshold bar here is the classification guide. A predicted score
below 50% is flagged as Critical, 50 to 64 as High Risk, 65 to 74 as
Medium, and 75 and above is considered Low or Stable.

You can see the system is currently training — the loading indicator
confirms the models are being built in the browser right now.

Once complete, the summary cards show how many staff fall into each
risk category, along with the percentage of the total team they represent.

Each alert card shows the staff member's current score, the predicted
score for next year, the change, and a confidence bar derived from the
model's training accuracy. The system also provides specific recommended
actions based on the severity of the risk.

Staff who are predicted to remain stable are grouped in this collapsed
section at the bottom — keeping the page focused on those who need
attention, while still allowing the supervisor to check on the safe staff
if needed."

--- TECHNICAL POINTS (if asked by lecturer) ---

  Model architecture:
    Input (normalised year index 0–1)
    → Dense layer: 8 neurons, ReLU activation
    → Dense layer: 4 neurons, ReLU activation
    → Dense layer: 1 neuron, Linear activation → predicted score

  Training:
    Optimiser: Adam (adaptive learning rate)
    Loss function: Mean Squared Error (MSE)
    Epochs: 200

  Confidence:
    Confidence = 100 − MAE (Mean Absolute Error on training data)
    Clamped between 50% and 99%

  Why neural network over linear regression?
    Linear regression only models straight-line trends.
    A neural network with ReLU activation can model curves, plateaus,
    and recoveries — more realistic for human performance data.

--- SCRIPT ---

"The Smart Training Recommendations feature automatically analyses each
staff member's KPI performance and identifies their specific skill gaps.

The system compares their category scores against defined thresholds.
A score below 50% in any category is flagged as a Critical gap,
below 70% as Moderate, and below 85% as Minor.

These gaps are then matched against a library of six training programs.
Each program has defined focus areas, and the system calculates a
compatibility score — shown here as the match percentage — based on
how well the program addresses this particular staff member's weaknesses.
The higher the gap severity, the higher the match score for a relevant program.

The priority level — High, Medium, or Low — is determined by the most
severe gap identified. This helps supervisors focus their resources on
the staff who need the most urgent intervention.

The supervisor can also filter by priority level or department to narrow
down the list.

And importantly, clicking Edit Comment here takes the supervisor directly
to that staff member's profile with the comment modal already open —
so they can update the training recommendation without having to navigate
manually."

--- TECHNICAL POINTS (if asked) ---

  Gap thresholds:
    < 50% → Critical (priority 3)
    < 70% → Moderate (priority 2)
    < 85% → Minor   (priority 1)

  Match score calculation:
    For each program's focus area that overlaps with a staff gap:
      Critical gap in that area → +40 match points
      Moderate gap             → +30 match points
      Minor gap                → +20 match points
    Normalised to 0–100%

  6 training programs:
    Sales Excellence Program (4 weeks)
    Customer Service Mastery (3 weeks)
    Leadership Fundamentals (6 weeks — requires score ≥ 85%)
    Operational Excellence (2 weeks)
    Time Management & Productivity (2 weeks)
    Advanced Product Knowledge (3 weeks)

  - Three anomaly types: Spike, Drop, Consecutive Decline

  - Narrative insight panel with actionable context
  - Staff selector dropdown — works for any staff member

--- SCRIPT ---

"The Anomaly Detection feature scans a staff member's performance history
and automatically identifies unusual patterns that a supervisor might miss
when looking at raw numbers.

The system detects three types of anomalies.
A Spike is a sudden increase of 5% or more in a single year — which could
indicate a training program working well, or a data entry issue worth reviewing.
A Drop is a sudden decrease of 5% or more — which typically signals something
went wrong and needs investigation.
A Consecutive Decline is when performance decreases for three or more years
in a row — the most serious pattern, indicating a long-term problem.

Each anomaly is marked directly on the chart so the supervisor can see
exactly when it occurred. Below the chart, each anomaly is described in
plain language — not just a number, but an explanation of what happened
and why it matters.

The narrative insight panel at the bottom ties everything together,
giving the supervisor a clear picture of this staff member's performance
story and what action to consider."

--- TECHNICAL POINTS (if asked) ---

  Detection logic:
    Spike:   change >= +5% between consecutive years
    Drop:    change <= -5% between consecutive years
    Decline: 3 or more consecutive years of decreasing score

  Narrative generation:
    Rule-based text templates filled with actual data values.
    Not AI-generated — deterministic based on the anomaly type and magnitude.

--- SCRIPT ---

"The Peer Comparison feature allows supervisors to benchmark any staff
member against their peers — helping identify performance gaps and
inform mentorship or development decisions.

I'll select Emma Wilson here, who is one of our at-risk staff members,
and compare her against the auto-selected best match.

The system finds the most similar peer based on overall score proximity
and displays both staff side by side. The scores are shown as proper
percentages so they are immediately meaningful to anyone reading them.

The radar chart here is particularly useful — it overlays both staff
members' KPI category scores on the same chart. The supervisor can
instantly see which specific areas one staff member is stronger or
weaker in compared to their peer. This is much more informative than
just comparing a single overall score.

The similarity score tells the supervisor how comparable these two
staff members are — a higher similarity means the comparison is more
meaningful.

Below the main comparison, the system also suggests other similar peers
that the supervisor might want to compare against.

Now let me demonstrate the Specific Staff Member option — this is a
feature we added to give supervisors more control. Instead of relying
on the auto-selection, the supervisor can manually choose who they want
to compare against. I'll select Alice Johnson, our top performer,
to show the contrast."

[Run comparison with Alice Johnson]

"You can now clearly see the performance gap between Emma and Alice
across every KPI category — which gives the supervisor a concrete,
data-driven basis for setting improvement targets."

--- TECHNICAL POINTS (if asked) ---

  Similarity calculation:
    similarity = 100 − |staff_score − peer_score|
    Peers within ±10% are considered similar.

  Comparison modes:
    Auto-select Best Match  — finds closest score match
    Top Performer           — compares against highest scorer in that year
    Team Average            — compares against team benchmark
    Specific Staff Member   — supervisor manually selects the peer

  Radar chart:
    Each axis = one KPI group (category average score)
    Two datasets overlaid: selected staff vs peer
    Scores on 1–5 scale (raw KPI scores, not weighted)

================================================================================
  OVERALL TALKING POINTS FOR ADVANCED ANALYTICS
================================================================================

If asked why these four features were chosen:

  "The four advanced analytics features were designed to address different
  aspects of performance management that go beyond simple reporting.

  Predictive Alerts addresses the future — giving supervisors early warning
  before a problem becomes critical.

  Training Recommendations addresses the solution — not just identifying
  who needs help, but specifically what kind of help they need.

  Anomaly Detection addresses the past — helping supervisors understand
  unusual events in a staff member's history that might otherwise go unnoticed.

  Peer Comparison addresses context — performance data is more meaningful
  when compared against similar colleagues rather than viewed in isolation."

If asked about the technology choices:

  "TensorFlow.js was chosen for Predictive Alerts because it allows
  machine learning to run directly in the browser without any server-side
  infrastructure. This keeps the system self-contained and deployable
  on a standard XAMPP setup without additional dependencies.

  The other three features use PHP-based algorithms — rule-based matching
  for training recommendations, statistical thresholds for anomaly detection,
  and score-based similarity for peer comparison. These approaches are
  transparent, explainable, and appropriate for the scale of the data."
